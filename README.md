# Vāṇī Sanskrit Pronunciation Coach — Version 8 Natural Voices

Version 8 keeps the successful Version 7 Acoustic Match system and adds a male/female reference-voice choice.

## What changed

- **Female** and **Male** options are available in **Settings → Reference audio**.
- The app uses a natural-sounding voice installed on the Mac rather than the earlier harsh bundled voice.
- It prefers Indian voices such as **Veena**, **Lekha**, or **Rishi** when they are installed. It can otherwise use natural Mac voices such as **Samantha** or **Alex**.
- The exact voice recording played to you is used for the pronunciation comparison.
- The slow-listening speed is unchanged from Version 7.

The Acoustic Match checker, complete-recording search, bird/background rejection, sound score, rhythm score, clarity score, microphone settings, progress and lessons are unchanged.

## Start on a Mac

1. Unzip the application.
2. Double-click `START-MAC.command`.
3. Keep the Terminal window open.
4. Open **Settings → Reference audio**.
5. Choose **Female** or **Male**.
6. Press **Test reference audio**.

The first use of a word with a selected voice can take a moment while macOS prepares and locally caches the recording. Later playback is immediate.

If macOS blocks `START-MAC.command`, right-click it and choose **Open**, or use **System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway**.

## Getting better Mac voices

If the app reports that a voice is unavailable, open macOS **System Settings**, search for **Spoken Content** or **VoiceOver voices**, and download an enhanced Indian or English male/female voice. Restart the application afterwards.

## Pronunciation scoring

- **Sound** compares the changing speech spectrum with the chosen reference voice.
- **Rhythm** compares syllable timing and energy changes.
- **Clarity** reflects how confidently the app isolated your voice from the recording.

The result is an educational acoustic-similarity percentage, not a formal Sanskrit linguistic assessment.
